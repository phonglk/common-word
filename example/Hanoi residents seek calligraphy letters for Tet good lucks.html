Hanoi residents seek calligraphy letters for Tet good lucks

Tuoi Tre News

Updated : 01/31/2017 08:23 GMT + 7

Calligraphers write the letters at the Temple of Literature in Hanoi on January 29, 2017.
Tuoi Tre
PrevNext
1

Thousands of people have gathered at a famous temple in Hanoi to be granted calligraphy letters, as part of a traditional ritual during the Lunar New Year (Tet) holiday.

Many residents arrived at the Temple of Literature in the Vietnamese capital to receive the letters written by skilled calligraphers, which are considered good luck during the Lunar New Year.

Visitors had to wait in long lines to buy tickets to the venue as well as to receive their good luck charms.

Minh Anh, a Hanoi resident, stated she had to wait for about 30 minutes to obtain a calligraphy letter for her daughter.

The calligraphers are scheduled to hand out their artistic works from 8:00 am to 6:00 pm every day from January 29 to February 5.

According to the traditional belief, receiving the calligraphy alphabets on the first days of the Lunar year will grant good luck during the entire year.

People coming from different walks of life are advised to select letters with different meanings.

Young pupils and students shall be given the letter ‘Tri’ and ‘Tai,’ which mean wit and talent, while entrepreneurs should choose the words ‘Phat’ (prospect) or ‘Loc’ (Luck).

Those who wish to sharpen their endurance will be given the word ‘Nhan,’ or patience, while people who want to be successful in life will receive ‘Thanh’ or ‘Dat,’ which mean to triumph.

Bamboo paper, on which the letters are written, are sold at the temple at VND100,000 (US$4.42) a scroll, about VND20,000 ($0.88) higher than previous years.

By 11:00 am on Sunday, several types of bamboo paper were out of stock due to the large number of buyers.
